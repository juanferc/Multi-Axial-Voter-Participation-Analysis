Multi-Axial Voter Participation Analysis

The Multi-Axial Voter Participation Analysis project explores the question of whether or not there is a connection between economic status and voter participation. Specifically, if there is higher voter participation in areas experiencing greater economic prosperity than in areas experiencing less economic prosperity. The project compares the rate of voter participation across 115 randomly selected zip codes and then charts that data against the median home price and educational attainment.

The Social Challenge

• The percentage of people who vote in the United States is low by international standards.

• Voting rates are unevenly distributed across socioeconomic groups, with much lower voting rates among the poor.

• Is higher education the key connection between higher incomes and voter turnout.
